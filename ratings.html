Rating All the Books I Read This Year
Title Author Rating Comments
A is for Arsenic Kathryn Harkup ★★★ Interesting but somewhat dry
Heaven Official's Blessing/天官赐福 墨香铜臭 ★★★★★ Very long (one million word count) Chinese webnovel full of romance, engaging plot, plot twists, and humor. 
Smoke Gets in Your Eyes Caitlin Doughty ★★★★★ Funny memoir about the author's first foray into the funeral industry while working at a crematorium.
Crooked House Agatha Christie ★★★★★ Cool plot twist
Silent Reading/默读 Priest ★★★★ Another very long Chinese crime thriller webnovel with a ton of suspense and somewhat convoluted plots
The Guest List Lucy Foley ★★★★ I stayed up all night to read it, and the payoff was 99% worth it.
The Kaleidescope of Death

Xī Zǐxù/西子绪

 ★★★ Very gory horror novel with a confusing and anticlimatic ending
Magpie Murders Anthony Horowitz ★★★ A creative story within a story murder mystery, but ultimately the reveal was lame. Author also seems to have a specific view of gay people. 
Moonflower Murders Anthony Horowitz ★★★★ Although better plotted than its predecessor, the story within a story aspect was lacking. Author again has some fixed ideas about gay people. 
How to Fight Presidents Daniel O'Brien ★ It was just kinda bad and unfunny and inaccurate. 
Jack and Lem David Pitts ★★★★★ Although writing isn't the most engaging, as the owner of a JFK plate, this was interesting look into an important figure in his life not often mentioned in history. 
Afterwords: Letters on the Death of Virginia Woolf Sybil Oldfield N/A A collection of letters to Leonard Woolf and Vanessa Bell on Virginia Woolf's deaths; Interesting
Golden Stage/黄金台 苍梧宾白 ★★★★ A court intrigue novel although the main focus is romance; I have since forgotten the plot
The Word is Murder Anthony Horowitz ★★★★ Good murder mystery with a pretty good twist! Author's stereotyping of gay people appears again.
The Sentence is Death Anthony Horowitz ★★★★ I already forgot the details but I remember being entertained! The author's bad representation of gay people strikes again.
A Line to Kill Anthony Horowitz ★★★ I guessed the killer halfway through. No mention of gay people at all this time. 
Thursday Murder Club Richard Osman ★★★ A funny murder mystery novel whose ending I found weak. 
Both Sides Now Peyton Thomas ★ Hate read since I don't usually read YA but I dislike this author. 
Life Among the Savages Shirley Jackson ★★★★★ Just funny anecdotes from a horror author about raising her four children in 1940s Vermont. 
We Two: Victoria and Albert Gillian Gill ★★★★ A really interesting book about the marriage of Queen Victoria and Prince Albert. 
Fight the Landord, Fall in Love 苍梧宾白 ★ Just didn't like it

I may have read more books this year but these are all I remember.
